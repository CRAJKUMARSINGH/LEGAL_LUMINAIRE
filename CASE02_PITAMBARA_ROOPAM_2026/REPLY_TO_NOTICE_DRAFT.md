## POINT-WISE REPLY

**2. Regarding Delay in Supply of Approved Drawings — Primary Breach by the Trust**

It is a matter of undisputed record that:

- The scheduled date of commencement of work was **01.05.2024**.
- The approved architectural/structural drawings for the project were supplied by the Trust to our firm only on **22.02.2025** — a delay of approximately **10 (ten) months** from the scheduled start date.
- This means that **two-thirds of the originally scheduled project period was consumed by the Trust's own failure** to provide the mandatory approved drawings.

It is a fundamental principle of construction contracts that the Contractor cannot be expected to commence or execute structural work without approved drawings. The Trust's failure to supply drawings in time is a **primary breach** of its obligations under the contract.

We call upon the Trust to explain how it expects our firm to complete the project within the original schedule when the Trust itself withheld the approved drawings for 10 months.

**3. Regarding Compliance with Technical and Statutory Requirements**

We categorically deny the allegation that our firm failed to maintain requisite technical staff, field testing laboratory, third-party quality assurance, or workman insurance. The facts are:

- Our firm has at all times maintained qualified technical/field engineering staff at the project site.
- A field testing laboratory has been operational and maintained throughout the project duration.
- Third-party quality assurance arrangements have been timely and regularly maintained.
- Workman insurance (as evidenced by the insurance documents enclosed herewith) has been duly arranged and maintained by our firm.

The fact that the project has progressed to its current stage is itself conclusive proof that all statutory and contractual compliances were in place. Without these, no meaningful construction could have been accomplished.

**4. Regarding the Incident of 19.11.2025 — Biased and Unilateral Assessment by the Trust**

We strongly object to the Trust's characterization of the incident of **19.11.2025** (hereinafter "the mishap") as being attributable to the absence of a field engineer.

- The cause of the mishap has **not been independently or expertly investigated** to date. No formal inquiry by a qualified structural/forensic expert has been conducted.
- The Trust's assessment of the cause is **unilateral, biased, and without any technical basis**.
- Even assuming (which is denied) that there was any momentary absence of a field engineer, this cannot be the sole or proximate cause of the mishap without a proper expert inquiry.
- More importantly, the Trust itself, **after the mishap, instructed our firm to stop all work** pending supply of revised drawings. This instruction was duly complied with by our firm.
- The Trust has **not provided the revised drawings to date**, thereby continuing its own breach and preventing our firm from resuming work.

The Trust cannot, on one hand, instruct stoppage of work pending revised drawings and, on the other hand, allege breach of contract by our firm for non-completion.

**5. Regarding Breach of Contract — Principle of Reciprocal Promises**

We draw the Trust's attention to the settled legal position under the **Indian Contract Act, 1872**, particularly:

- **Section 51** — When a contract consists of reciprocal promises, the promisor need not perform his promise unless the other party is ready and willing to perform his reciprocal promise.
- **Section 53** — When a party to a contract prevents the other from performing, the contract becomes voidable at the option of the party so prevented.
- **Section 55** — Time being of the essence: where a party fails to perform within the agreed time, the other party is entitled to compensation for the resulting delay.

The Trust's obligation to supply approved drawings is a **condition precedent** to our firm's obligation to execute the structural work. By withholding drawings for 10 months and thereafter failing to supply revised drawings post-mishap, the Trust has:

(a) Prevented our firm from performing;
(b) Committed a continuous breach of contract;
(c) Rendered the original completion schedule impossible to adhere to.

**We have not breached the contract. It is the Trust that has breached the contract, and the breach is ongoing.**

**6. Regarding Time Extension**

In view of the above, our firm is entitled to and hereby formally demands a **provisional time extension** commensurate with:

- The period of work stoppage post-mishap (19.11.2025 onwards) due to non-supply of revised drawings.

Unless and until the revised drawings are supplied and a formal time extension is granted, our firm shall not be bound to complete the work within the original scheduled completion date, and no penalty, liquidated damages, or adverse action shall be maintainable against our firm for the period of delay attributable to the Trust.
