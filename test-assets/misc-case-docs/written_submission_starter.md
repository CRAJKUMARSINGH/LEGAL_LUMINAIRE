### **SAMPLE COMPLETED SECTION**

#### **SAMPLE ARGUMENT**

**ARGUMENT 1**: The plaintiff has established a prima facie case for breach of contract

**Legal Basis**:
- Section 73 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Compensation for loss or damage caused by breach of contract
- Section 75 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Party injured need not prove actual loss

**Factual Foundation**:
- Contract dated January 15, 2024 between parties
- Breach occurred on April 30, 2024
- Plaintiff suffered quantifiable losses of INR 25,00,000/-
- Evidence of breach and losses annexed

**Jurisprudential Support**:
- M/s. ABC vs. XYZ (2019) 5 SCC 456 - Established principles for breach of contract
- M/s. PQR vs. LMN (2020) 8 SCC 789 - Compensation principles
- M/s. DEF vs. GHI (2021) 3 SCC 234 - Evidence requirements

**Analysis**:
The plaintiff has established all essential elements of breach of contract:
1. Existence of valid contract
2. Breach by defendant
3. Causation of loss
4. Quantifiable damages

**Counter-Arguments**:
Anticipated argument that contract was void due to lack of consideration. This is without merit as consideration was clearly established and evidenced.

**Conclusion**:
The plaintiff has established a prima facie case and is entitled to relief as prayed for.

### **WRITING GUIDELINES**

#### **STRUCTURE**:
- Use clear headings and subheadings
- Maintain logical flow
- Use numbered paragraphs for easy reference
- Include table of contents for longer submissions

#### **LANGUAGE**:
- Use formal, professional language
- Avoid colloquialisms
- Be precise and concise
- Use legal terminology correctly

#### **CITATIONS**:
- Follow proper citation format
- Include full case citations
- Include pin-point citations where relevant
- Use consistent citation style

#### **ARGUMENTATION**:
- Present arguments logically
- Support arguments with authority
- Address counter-arguments
- Conclude each argument clearly

#### **COMPLIANCE**:
- Follow court rules and procedures
- Adhere to page limits
- Include required annexures
- Proper verification and signing
