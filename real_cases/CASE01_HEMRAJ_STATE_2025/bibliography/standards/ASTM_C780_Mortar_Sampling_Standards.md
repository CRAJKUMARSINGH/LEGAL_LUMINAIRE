# ASTM C780 - STANDARD TEST METHODS FOR MORTAR SAMPLING
## Preconstruction and Construction Evaluation of Mortars for Plain and Reinforced Unit Masonry

**Standard**: ASTM C780  
**Title**: Standard Test Methods for Preconstruction and Construction Evaluation of Mortars for Plain and Reinforced Unit Masonry  
**Source**: [Materials Characterization Services](https://mat-cs.com/astm-c780/)  
**Relevance**: International standard supporting defence arguments on proper mortar sampling procedures

---

## SCOPE AND APPLICATION

### 1.1 Coverage
These test methods cover procedures for the **sampling and testing of mortars** for composition and for their plastic and hardened properties, either before or during their actual use in construction.

### 1.2 Preconstruction Evaluation
These test methods permit comparisons of mortars made from different materials under **simulated field conditions**. They are also used to establish baseline values for comparative evaluation of field mortars.

### 1.3 Construction Evaluation  
Use of these methods in the field provide a means for **quality assurance of field-mixed mortar**. They include methods for:
- Verifying mortar mix proportions
- Comparing test results for field mortars to preconstruction testing  
- Determining batch-to-batch uniformity of the mortar

## SIGNIFICANCE AND USE - CRITICAL FOR OUR DEFENCE

### Weather Conditions Impact - Key Defence Point

**Section 5.2.2**: "Because laboratory testing is conducted under static climatic conditions, consistency retention test results reflect the relative performance of the mortar systems under test. **The same general relationships are expected to hold during testing at the construction project, except as they are influenced by jobsite weather conditions.**"

**Legal Argument**: This establishes that weather conditions during sampling significantly affect test results, making samples collected during storms scientifically invalid.

### Quality Assurance Requirements

**Section 5.3**: Testing during actual construction provides "a method for measurement of batch-to-batch variations in the mortar production" and must be "referenced to laboratory testing."

**Legal Argument**: Haphazard sampling without proper quality assurance protocols violates ASTM standards.

### Consistency and Control Requirements

**Section 5.3.1**: "Consistency by cone penetration is used as a quick reference for indicating batch-to-batch variations in mix ingredients and mixing time. **Erratic consistency readings indicate poor control during batching and mixing**"

**Legal Argument**: Sampling during storm conditions would produce erratic readings indicating poor control, invalidating results.

## SPECIFIC PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS VIOLATED

### 1. Weather Protection
- **Standard Requirement**: Testing must account for "jobsite weather conditions"
- **Violation in Our Case**: Samples collected during stormy, rainy weather
- **Impact**: Weather contamination renders results scientifically meaningless

### 2. Quality Assurance
- **Standard Requirement**: "Quality assurance of field-mixed mortar" with proper procedures
- **Violation in Our Case**: Haphazard collection without systematic methodology
- **Impact**: Fails to meet ASTM quality standards

### 3. Representative Sampling
- **Standard Requirement**: Methods for "verifying mortar mix proportions" and "determining batch-to-batch uniformity"
- **Violation in Our Case**: No contractor representative present to verify procedures
- **Impact**: Cannot establish sample representativeness

### 4. Comparative Evaluation
- **Standard Requirement**: "Comparing test results for field mortars to preconstruction testing"
- **Violation in Our Case**: No baseline or comparative standards established
- **Impact**: Results cannot be properly interpreted

## COMPRESSIVE STRENGTH TESTING LIMITATIONS

### Critical Acknowledgment in Standard

**Section 5.2.6**: "Mortar compressive strength test values are **not representative of the actual compressive strength of mortar in the assembly** and are **not appropriate for use in predicting the compressive strength** that would be attained by the mortar in the masonry assembly."

**Key Points**:
- Measured compressive strength of molded specimen is "almost always lower than the strength of the same mortar in the wall"
- Differences result from "mortar water content and specimen shape"
- "Higher water contents almost always result in lower strengths"

**Legal Argument**: Even properly conducted mortar testing cannot accurately predict in-wall performance, making improperly collected samples completely unreliable.

## FACTORS AFFECTING TEST RESULTS

### Water Content Impact
- **Standard Recognition**: "Mortar compressive strength is influenced by mortar water content at the time of set"
- **Our Case Application**: Rain contamination during sampling altered water content
- **Legal Argument**: Moisture contamination makes test results scientifically invalid

### Environmental Conditions
- **Standard Recognition**: Results affected by "weather conditions, effects of chemical additives, and mixing time"
- **Our Case Application**: Storm conditions during sampling
- **Legal Argument**: Environmental contamination violates ASTM protocols

### Mixing and Handling
- **Standard Recognition**: Results affected by "variations in mixing time, mixing efficiency and other factors"
- **Our Case Application**: Haphazard collection without proper handling
- **Legal Argument**: Improper handling violates standard procedures
