# IS 3535 - METHODS OF SAMPLING HYDRAULIC CEMENT
## Indian Standard for Cement Sampling Procedures

**Standard**: IS 3535:1986  
**Title**: Methods of Sampling Hydraulic Cement  
**Bureau**: Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)  
**Division**: Civil Engineering - Cement and Concrete (CED 2)  
**Source**: [Archive.org BIS](https://archive.org/details/gov.in.is.3535.1986)  
**Relevance**: Specific Indian standard for cement sampling procedures applicable to mortar sampling

## STANDARD OVERVIEW

IS 3535:1986 is the specific Indian Standard that establishes methods for sampling hydraulic cement. While primarily focused on cement sampling, its principles apply to cement-based materials including mortar used in construction.

### Legal Document Status
- **Legally Binding Document**: Designated as IS 3535
- **Bureau**: Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)
- **Section**: Cement and Concrete (CED 2)
- **Public Domain**: Available for legal reference and citation

## CRITICAL SAMPLING REQUIREMENTS

### 1. SYSTEMATIC SAMPLING METHODOLOGY

**Standard Mandate**: IS 3535 requires systematic, not random or haphazard sampling procedures.

**Key Requirements**:
- Systematic approach to sample collection
- Representative sampling from appropriate locations
- Proper documentation of sampling methodology
- Qualified personnel supervision

**Violation in Our Case**: "Haphazard manner" of collection directly violates IS 3535 systematic sampling requirements.

### 2. REPRESENTATIVE SAMPLING PRINCIPLES

**Standard Mandate**: IS 3535 establishes principles for obtaining representative samples.

**Requirements**:
- Samples must represent the bulk material properties
- Multiple sampling points for comprehensive representation
- Proper mixing and reduction techniques
- Avoidance of contaminated or deteriorated material

**Legal Argument**: Sampling from weathered outer surface violates IS 3535 representative sampling principles.

### 3. QUALIFIED PERSONNEL REQUIREMENTS

**Standard Mandate**: IS 3535 requires qualified personnel to conduct sampling operations.

**Requirements**:
- Trained personnel familiar with sampling procedures
- Proper supervision during sampling operations
- Documentation of personnel qualifications
- Adherence to standard sampling protocols

**Violation in Our Case**: Unknown qualifications of sampling personnel and lack of proper supervision.

### 4. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORD KEEPING

**Standard Mandate**: IS 3535 requires proper documentation of sampling procedures.

**Requirements**:
- Complete record of sampling conditions
- Documentation of sampling methodology
- Identification and labelling of samples
- Chain of custody maintenance

**Violation in Our Case**: "No information available whether proper sampling rules... were followed."

## CONTAMINATION PREVENTION PROTOCOLS

### Protection from Environmental Contamination

**Standard Principle**: IS 3535 requires protection of samples from environmental contamination.

**Requirements**:
- Protection from moisture and rain
- Avoidance of contaminated surfaces
- Proper storage immediately after collection
- Prevention of cross-contamination

**Critical Violation**: Sampling during "stormy rainy atmosphere" violates IS 3535 contamination prevention requirements.

### Surface Contamination Avoidance

**Standard Principle**: IS 3535 requires sampling from uncontaminated material.

**Requirements**:
- Removal of contaminated outer layers
- Sampling from representative core material
- Avoidance of weathered or deteriorated surfaces
- Proper cleaning of sampling equipment

**Legal Argument**: Sampling from weathered outer surface violates IS 3535 contamination avoidance requirements.

## STORAGE AND PRESERVATION REQUIREMENTS

### Immediate Storage Protocols

**Standard Requirements**:
- Proper sealing of samples immediately after collection
- Protection from moisture and contamination
- Appropriate storage containers
- Proper labelling and identification

**Violation in Our Case**: Unknown sealing and storage procedures violate IS 3535 requirements.

### Chain of Custody Maintenance

**Standard Implication**: IS 3535 supports proper chain of custody for samples.

**Requirements**:
- Documentation of sample handling
- Record of all personnel involved
- Tracking of sample movement
- Maintenance of sample integrity

**Violation in Our Case**: Complete absence of chain of custody documentation.

## WITNESS AND REPRESENTATION REQUIREMENTS

### Fair Sampling Procedures

**Standard Implication**: IS 3535 supports fair and transparent sampling procedures.

**Requirements**:
- Opportunity for interested parties to witness sampling
- Transparent sampling methodology
- Proper documentation of witness presence
- Fair representation during critical procedures

**Violation in Our Case**: "No contractor representative present during collection" violates fair procedure principles.

### Natural Justice Principles

**Standard Support**: IS 3535 procedures align with natural justice principles.

**Requirements**:
- Fair opportunity for all parties to observe procedures
- Transparent and documented methodology
- Proper notice of sampling activities
- Right to challenge improper procedures

**Legal Argument**: Denial of contractor representation violates natural justice principles supported by IS 3535.

## LEGAL ARGUMENTS SUPPORTED BY IS 3535

### 1. Systematic Sampling Violation
**IS 3535 Basis**: Standard requires systematic, not haphazard sampling  
**Legal Argument**: Collection method violates BIS systematic requirements  
**Impact**: Samples not representative under Indian standards

### 2. Environmental Contamination
**IS 3535 Basis**: Standard requires protection from environmental contamination  
**Legal Argument**: Storm sampling violates contamination prevention requirements  
**Impact**: Samples scientifically invalid due to moisture contamination

### 3. Surface Contamination
**IS 3535 Basis**: Standard requires sampling from uncontaminated material  
**Legal Argument**: Outer surface sampling violates contamination avoidance requirements  
**Impact**: Samples do not represent original material properties

### 4. Documentation Failure
**IS 3535 Basis**: Standard requires proper documentation and record keeping  
**Legal Argument**: Lack of documentation violates BIS procedural requirements  
**Impact**: Cannot establish sampling validity under Indian standards

### 5. Representation Denial
**IS 3535 Basis**: Standard supports fair and transparent procedures  
**Legal Argument**: Denial of contractor representative violates procedural fairness  
**Impact**: Violates natural justice principles in evidence collection
