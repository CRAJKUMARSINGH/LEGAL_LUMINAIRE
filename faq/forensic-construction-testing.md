### **Q2: WHAT MATERIALS MUST BE RESERVED FOR FORENSIC INVESTIGATION?**

**ANSWER**: 
Forensic investigation requires comprehensive material preservation:

**CONCRETE SAMPLES**:
- **Core Samples**: Minimum 100mm diameter, full-depth cores
- **Retention Period**: 5-10 years for major structures
- **Storage Conditions**: Climate-controlled, labeled containers
- **Documentation**: Exact location, date, and collection method

**CEMENT SAMPLES**:
- **Original Bags**: Retain unopened cement bags from same batch
- **Retention Period**: 5 years for forensic investigation
- **Storage Conditions**: Moisture-proof, sealed containers
- **Testing Parameters**: Chemical analysis, strength verification

**MORTAR SAMPLES**:
- **In-situ Samples**: Extracted mortar from joints
- **Retention Period**: 5 years for investigation
- **Storage Conditions**: Similar to concrete samples
- **Documentation**: Joint location, mix proportions

### **Q4: WHAT ARE THE FORENSIC TESTING STANDARDS AND PROCEDURES?**

**ANSWER**: 
Forensic testing follows specialized standards:

**TESTING STANDARDS**:
- **ASTM C823**: Standard practice for examination and sampling of hardened concrete
- **ASTM C42**: Standard test method for obtaining and testing drilled cores
- **IS 13311**: Non-destructive testing of concrete
- **IS 1199**: Methods of sampling and analysis of concrete

**FORENSIC PROCEDURES**:
- **Petrographic Analysis**: Microscopic examination of concrete
- **Chemical Analysis**: Chemical composition testing
- **Non-Destructive Testing**: Ultrasonic, rebound hammer testing
- **Destructive Testing**: Core strength testing

### **Q13: HOW DOES FORENSIC TESTING DIFFER FROM ROUTINE QUALITY CONTROL?**

**ANSWER**: 
Key differences include:

**PURPOSE**:
- **Forensic Testing**: Investigation of failures or defects
- **Quality Control**: Verification of compliance with standards

**TIMING**:
- **Forensic Testing**: Post-construction, after problems identified
- **Quality Control**: During construction, before problems occur

**SCOPE**:
- **Forensic Testing**: Comprehensive investigation of specific issues
- **Quality Control**: Routine verification of all materials

**LEGAL IMPLICATIONS**:
- **Forensic Testing**: Significant legal implications and evidence requirements
- **Quality Control**: Limited legal implications, primarily contractual
