## Oral Argument Paragraphs (Ready-to-Use — 5 Paras)

**Para 1 — Opening (19 Variation Orders — Scope Creep):**
"My Lord, the original contract was for 85,000 plants and 28 kilometres of internal roads. USCL issued 19 Variation Orders adding 42,000 extra plants, 6.5 kilometres of additional roads, and fountain and lakefront development. All 19 orders were issued in writing by the Engineer-in-Charge. All were executed by the Claimant. CPWD GCC Clause 12 is unambiguous: variations ordered in writing are binding on the Employer. The total variation claim of ₹6.85 Crore is supported by 19 written orders and joint measurements. USCL cannot now deny payment."

**Para 2 — Water Supply Failure:**
"My Lord, Special Conditions Clause 15 expressly provides that USCL shall supply treated water for irrigation. USCL stopped supplying water from September 2024. The plantation survival rate dropped to 43% — against the contractual requirement of 85%. The Claimant was forced to replace 38,000 plants twice at its own cost. USCL's own water supply log — which is on record — confirms that supply was stopped. The Claimant cannot be held liable for survival failure caused by the Employer's own failure to supply water. The replacement cost of ₹3.15 Crore is fully justified."

**Para 3 — Payment Delays:**
"My Lord, seven Running Account Bills have been pending for 4 to 11 months. CPWD GCC Clause 7 mandates payment within 30 days. The non-payment caused a severe cash-flow crisis that disrupted the Claimant's ability to purchase plants and materials. The Claimant is entitled to the principal amount of ₹3.85 Crore plus interest at 12% per annum — totalling ₹4.25 Crore."

**Para 4 — Termination Illegal:**
"My Lord, USCL terminated the contract citing slow progress of 64% and poor plantation survival. But the slow progress was caused by 19 Variation Orders that massively expanded the scope. The poor survival was caused by USCL's own failure to supply water. USCL's own Standing Committee Minutes recommend termination but are conspicuously silent about the 19 Variation Orders and the water supply failure. The Rajasthan High Court in RSMML v. Contractor has held that a contractor cannot be penalised for delays caused by the Employer's own defaults. The termination is illegal."

**Para 5 — Closing:**
"My Lord, the Claimant has suffered losses of ₹20.75 Crore due to USCL's scope creep, payment delays, and failure to supply treated water. Five of the six claims are VERIFIED with Fact-Fit scores of 85-96%. The sixth claim (loss of profit) is SECONDARY and presented with appropriate qualification. We pray for an award of ₹20.75 Crore with interest at 12% per annum, declaration that the termination is illegal, release of the Performance Bank Guarantee, and costs of arbitration."
