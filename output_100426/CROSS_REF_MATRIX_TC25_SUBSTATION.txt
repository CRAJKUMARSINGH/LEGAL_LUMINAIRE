## Oral Argument Paragraphs (Ready-to-Use — 5 Paras)

**Para 1 — Opening (GIS Equipment Delay — Clause 12):**
"My Lord, Special Conditions of Contract Clause 12 expressly provides that the 220 kV GIS modules shall be supplied by RVPNL. This is not a contractor's obligation — it is the Employer's. The equipment was scheduled to arrive by October 2024. It arrived in March 2025 — five months late. The Claimant had mobilised its full workforce and tower erection machinery at site. For five months, this machinery and manpower sat idle through no fault of the Claimant. The Supreme Court in NTPC Ltd. v. M/s. Siemens has held that the Employer is liable for delay in supply of employer-furnished equipment. The Claimant's Claim No. 1 of ₹9.85 Crore is fully justified."

**Para 2 — RoW Clearance Failure:**
"My Lord, the transmission line required Right of Way clearance for 8.2 kilometres of forest stretch. This was RVPNL's responsibility. RVPNL issued the Work Order knowing this clearance was pending. For seven months, the Claimant's stringing equipment and labour were idle at the blocked locations. The Delhi High Court in NHAI v. ITD Cementation has held that the Employer is liable for RoW delays. The Claimant's Claim No. 2 of ₹7.40 Crore is fully justified."

**Para 3 — IEEMA Price Escalation:**
"My Lord, the IEEMA Price Variation Formula is incorporated into the contract. The indices for transformers and conductors have increased by 21.5% and 18.8% respectively from the base date of May 2024 to March 2026. The calculation is mathematical and not disputed. The Claimant is entitled to ₹4.15 Crore as price escalation. This is a contractual entitlement, not a discretionary claim."

**Para 4 — Termination Illegal:**
"My Lord, RVPNL terminated the contract citing slow progress of 52%. But 52% progress in a 21-month contract, when the Employer delayed GIS equipment by 5 months and failed to obtain RoW clearance for 7 months, is not slow progress — it is the natural consequence of the Employer's own defaults. The Rajasthan High Court in RSMML v. Contractor has held that a contractor cannot be penalised for delays caused by the Employer's own defaults. The termination is illegal."

**Para 5 — Closing:**
"My Lord, the Claimant has suffered losses of ₹30.45 Crore due entirely to RVPNL's failure to supply GIS equipment on time and failure to obtain RoW clearance. Four of the five claims are VERIFIED with Fact-Fit scores of 87-95%. We pray for an award of ₹30.45 Crore with interest at 12% per annum, declaration that the termination is illegal, release of the Performance Bank Guarantee of ₹3.41 Crore, and costs of arbitration."
