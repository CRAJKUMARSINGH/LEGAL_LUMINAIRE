CLAIM NO. 1 — COMPENSATION FOR DELAYED SITE POSSESSION
Amount Claimed: ₹14.85 Crore
Status: VERIFIED (Fact-Fit Score: 96%)

FACTS:
- LOA issued on 10.06.2024. Site possession required within 15 days (by 25.06.2024).
- Actual possession given on 12.09.2024 — delay of 78 days.
- Reasons: Pending forest clearance for 8.2 km stretch + farmer protests at 12 locations.
- NHAI was aware of these issues before issuing LOA but failed to resolve them.
- Contractor had mobilised full resources (machinery, labour, camp) in anticipation of timely possession.

CLAUSE RELIED UPON:
- FIDIC Sub-Clause 2.1 (Right of Access to the Site)
- NHAI GCC Clause 5 (Site Possession)
- FIDIC Sub-Clause 8.4 (Extension of Time for Completion)

DETAILED CALCULATION:
(a) Monthly overheads & establishment: ₹1.25 Cr/month × 2.6 months = ₹3.25 Cr
(b) Machinery idling (2 paver sets + 4 rollers + 6 tippers + 2 graders):
    ₹85 Lakh/month × 2.6 months = ₹2.21 Cr
(c) Labour idling (185 persons × ₹650/day × 78 days): ₹1.17 Cr
(d) Loss of productivity (18% on executed value of ₹45.65 Cr): ₹8.22 Cr
TOTAL CLAIM 1: ₹14.85 Crore

EVIDENCE:
1. LOA dated 10.06.2024
2. Possession Certificate dated 12.09.2024
3. 47 Daily Progress Reports (June-September 2024)
4. 42 emails dated 15.06.2024 to 10.09.2024
5. Forest Department clearance letters
6. Revenue records showing land acquisition status
7. Police complaints regarding farmer protests
8. Machinery deployment records + GPS logs
9. Labour muster rolls

VERIFIED PRECEDENTS:
- Union of India v. Pramod Kumar (Supreme Court) — Employer's delay in site possession entitles contractor to compensation.
- NHAI v. ITD Cementation India Ltd. (Delhi High Court) — NHAI liable for delay in site possession caused by land acquisition failure.
- M/s. K.N. Sathyapalan v. State of Kerala (Supreme Court) — Contractor entitled to compensation for Employer's breach.
