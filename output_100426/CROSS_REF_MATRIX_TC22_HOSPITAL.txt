## Oral Argument Paragraphs (Ready-to-Use — 5 Paras)

**Para 1 — Opening (Site Handover Delay):**
"My Lord, the Employer issued the Work Order on 05.05.2024 and was contractually bound under Clause 5 of CPWD GCC 2020 to hand over the site within 15 days. The site was not handed over until 20.05.2024 — a delay of 45 days. The Claimant had mobilised 62 workers and 3 sets of machinery at its own cost. The Employer's breach is admitted in its own Possession Certificate. The Supreme Court in M/s. K.N. Sathyapalan v. State of Kerala has held that such delay entitles the contractor to full compensation. The Claimant's Claim No. 1 of ₹4.82 Crore is therefore fully justified."

**Para 2 — Variation Orders:**
"My Lord, the Employer issued three Variation Orders — for the OT slab, ICU wing, and generator room — all in writing, all executed by the Claimant, all jointly measured. The Employer cannot now deny payment. CPWD GCC Clause 12 is unambiguous: variations ordered in writing by the Engineer-in-Charge are binding on the Employer. The total variation claim of ₹6.15 Crore is supported by three written orders and three joint measurement books. There is no defence to this claim."

**Para 3 — Termination Illegal:**
"My Lord, the Employer terminated the contract on 15.01.2026 citing slow progress. But the Employer had itself granted two Extensions of Time — EOT-1 for 90 days and EOT-2 for 120 days — precisely because the delay was caused by the Employer's own defaults. A party cannot grant an EOT acknowledging its own default and then terminate for the same delay. The Rajasthan High Court in RSMML v. Contractor (30 March 2026) has held that a contractor cannot be penalised for delays that the State's own records acknowledged. The termination is illegal and must be set aside."

**Para 4 — Payment Delays:**
"My Lord, Running Account Bills 3, 4, and 5 aggregating ₹8.45 Crore have been pending for 4 to 7 months. CPWD GCC Clause 7 mandates payment within 30 days. The Employer's own Standing Committee Minutes of 08.01.2026 directed the Finance Department to process these bills within 15 days — an admission of liability. Yet the bills remain unpaid. The Claimant is entitled to the principal amount plus interest at 12% per annum."

**Para 5 — Closing (Performance Bank Guarantee):**
"My Lord, the Employer has threatened to encash the Performance Bank Guarantee of ₹2.43 Crore. The Supreme Court in Hindustan Construction Co. v. Union of India has held that a Bank Guarantee cannot be encashed when the termination itself is under challenge. We pray that this Tribunal direct the Employer to maintain status quo on the Bank Guarantee pending the final award. The Claimant has a strong prima facie case on all grounds."
