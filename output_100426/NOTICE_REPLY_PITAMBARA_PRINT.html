Sir/Madam,

We, M/s Roopam Construction (hereinafter the Contractor), hereby tender this formal reply to the legal notice issued by your Trust dated [Date of Notice], received by us on [Date of Receipt].

At the outset, we strongly deny and refute each and every allegation as being false, frivolous, misconceived, and contrary to the actual facts and the terms of the contract. We reply point-wise as under:

Preliminary Objection

1. Notice is Premature, Misconceived, and Mala Fide. The Trust itself is in continuous breach of its primary contractual obligations and cannot take shelter behind a legal notice to cover its own defaults. The notice is therefore liable to be withdrawn forthwith.

Point-Wise Reply

2. Regarding Delay in Supply of Approved Drawings - Primary Breach by the Trust.

  • The scheduled date of commencement of work was 01.05.2024.
  • The approved drawings were supplied by the Trust only on 22.02.2025 - a delay of approximately 10 (ten) months.
  • Two-thirds of the originally scheduled project period was consumed by the Trust own failure to provide the mandatory approved drawings.

Supply of approved drawings is a condition precedent to structural work. The Trust failure constitutes a primary breach of its contractual obligations. We call upon the Trust to explain how it expects our firm to complete the project within the original schedule when the Trust itself withheld the approved drawings for 10 months.

3. Regarding Compliance with Technical and Statutory Requirements. We categorically deny the allegation of non-compliance. Our firm has at all times maintained: qualified technical/field engineering staff; an operational field testing laboratory; third-party quality assurance arrangements; and workman insurance - as evidenced by the insurance documents enclosed herewith. The progress of the project to its current stage is itself conclusive proof that all statutory and contractual compliances were in place.

4. Regarding the Incident of 19.11.2025 - Biased and Unilateral Assessment by the Trust.

  • The cause of the mishap has not been independently or expertly investigated to date. No formal inquiry by a qualified structural/forensic expert has been conducted.
  • The Trust assessment is unilateral, biased, and without any technical basis.
  • After the mishap, the Trust itself instructed our firm to stop all work pending supply of revised drawings - which have not been provided to date.

The Trust cannot instruct stoppage of work pending revised drawings and simultaneously allege breach of contract for non-completion.

5. Regarding Breach of Contract - Principle of Reciprocal Promises.

  • Section 51, Indian Contract Act, 1872 - The promisor need not perform unless the other party is ready and willing to perform its reciprocal promise.
  • Section 53 - When a party prevents the other from performing, the contract becomes voidable at the option of the party so prevented.
  • Section 55 - A party failing to perform within the agreed time is liable for compensation for the resulting delay.
We have not breached the contract. It is the Trust that has breached the contract, and the breach is ongoing.

6. Regarding Time Extension - Formal Demand. Our firm hereby formally demands a provisional time extension commensurate with: (a) the 10-month delay in supply of original approved drawings; and (b) the period of work stoppage from 19.11.2025 onwards due to non-supply of revised drawings. No penalty, liquidated damages, or adverse action shall be maintainable against our firm for the period of delay attributable to the Trust.
