## Oral Argument Paragraphs (Ready-to-Use — 5 Paras)

**Para 1 — Opening (FIDIC 4.12 — Unforeseeable Conditions):**
"My Lord, FIDIC Sub-Clause 4.12 provides that where a contractor encounters physical conditions which are unforeseeable, the contractor is entitled to an extension of time and additional payment. The tender bore logs showed soft alluvium to 8 metres depth. The actual foundation level revealed massive hard rock at 2-4 metres and swelling Montmorillonite clay at 6-10 metres. The IIT Jodhpur expert report — which is on record — confirms that these conditions were unforeseeable by an experienced contractor. The Claimant is entitled to ₹18.75 Crore under FIDIC 4.12."

**Para 2 — Inadequate Site Investigation by WRD:**
"My Lord, the Employer conducted only 4 boreholes for a dam foundation of 450 metres length. IS 6512:1987 Clause 4 mandates adequate foundation investigation. The inadequacy of the site investigation is the Employer's own failure. The Employer cannot issue a tender with inadequate bore logs, invite contractors to price on that basis, and then refuse to pay when the actual conditions differ. The Employer's own negligence in site investigation is the root cause of this dispute."

**Para 3 — Design Changes Unpaid:**
"My Lord, the Employer issued two design changes in writing — the revised foundation design on 15.11.2024 and the spillway redesign on 01.01.2025. Both were executed by the Claimant on the Employer's written instructions. FIDIC Sub-Clause 13.1 is unambiguous: variations ordered by the Engineer are binding on the Employer. The total design change claim of ₹12.60 Crore is supported by two written instructions and joint measurements. There is no defence to this claim."

**Para 4 — Termination Illegal:**
"My Lord, the Employer terminated the contract citing slow progress of 29%. But the slow progress was caused entirely by the unforeseeable geological conditions and the Employer's own design changes — both of which are the Employer's responsibility under FIDIC 4.12 and 13.1. The Rajasthan High Court in RSMML v. Contractor has held that a contractor cannot be penalised for delays caused by the Employer's own defaults. The termination is illegal and must be set aside."

**Para 5 — Closing:**
"My Lord, the Claimant has suffered losses of ₹54.85 Crore due to unforeseeable geological conditions and the Employer's own design changes. Four of the five claims are VERIFIED with Fact-Fit scores of 81-97%. The fifth claim (loss of profit) is SECONDARY and presented with appropriate qualification. We pray for an award of ₹54.85 Crore with interest at 12% per annum, declaration that the termination is illegal, release of the Performance Bank Guarantee of ₹4.37 Crore, and costs of arbitration."
