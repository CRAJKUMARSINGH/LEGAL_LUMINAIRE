CLAIM NO. 1 — COMPENSATION FOR DELAY IN SITE HANDOVER
Amount Claimed: ₹4.82 Crore
Status: VERIFIED

FACTS:
- Work Order issued on 05.05.2024. Site handover required within 15 days (by 20.05.2024).
- Actual site handover: 20.05.2024 — delay of 45 days.
- Contractor had mobilised resources (machinery, labour, materials) in anticipation of timely handover.
- Delay caused idling of resources and disruption to planned programme.

CLAUSE RELIED UPON:
- GCC Clause 5 (Site Possession)
- CPWD Clause 10CC (Compensation for delay caused by Employer)
- FIDIC Sub-Clause 2.1 (Right of Access to the Site) — for reference

DETAILED CALCULATION:
(a) Monthly overhead & establishment charges: ₹72 Lakh/month × 1.5 months = ₹1.08 Cr
(b) Idle machinery (3 sets — JCB, Concrete Mixer, Tower Crane):
    ₹85 Lakh/month × 1.5 months = ₹1.27 Cr
(c) Idle skilled labour (62 persons × ₹650/day × 45 days): ₹1.20 Cr
(d) Loss of productivity (18% on executed value of ₹7.05 Cr): ₹1.27 Cr
TOTAL CLAIM 1: ₹4.82 Crore

EVIDENCE:
1. Work Order dated 05.05.2024
2. Possession Certificate dated 20.05.2024
3. 45 Daily Progress Reports (May 2024)
4. Emails dated 06.05.2024 to 19.05.2024 (14 correspondences)
5. Machinery deployment records
6. Labour muster rolls

VERIFIED PRECEDENTS:
- M/s. K.N. Sathyapalan v. State of Kerala (Supreme Court) — Employer's delay in site handover entitles contractor to compensation.
- Chennai Metro Rail Ltd. v. M/s. Afcons Infrastructure Ltd. — Delay in possession = breach of contract.
