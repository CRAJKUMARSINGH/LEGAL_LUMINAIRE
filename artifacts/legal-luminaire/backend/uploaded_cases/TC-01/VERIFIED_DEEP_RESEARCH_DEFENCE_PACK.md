## 3) Defence architecture (for drafting pages)

### Ground A: Foundational evidentiary break
- No complete chain-of-custody narrative (collection -> sealing -> transport -> receipt -> testing -> storage).
- Relief sought: forensic report treated as unreliable for charge framing.

### Ground B: Sampling integrity failure
- Rain/storm exposure at collection stage causes moisture-variable contamination risk.
- Haphazard, non-representative collection defeats inferential value of lab result.

### Ground C: Natural justice / fair opportunity
- Absence of contractor representative during collection/testing process.
- Defence right to observe, contest, and seek parallel testing was diluted.

### Ground D: Section 45 Evidence Act reliability attack
- Expert opinion cannot cure foundational defects in source sample quality.
- Ask court to treat lab opinion as weak corroborative material, not primary incriminating basis.

### Ground E: Causation gap in criminal negligence
- Even if quality variance is alleged, prosecution must show proximate nexus between accused act and collapse outcome.
- Alternate causes (weather event, maintenance, post-construction factors) must be excluded.

## 5) Court-ready draft paragraphs (safe baseline)

### Para 1 - chain of custody
It is submitted that the prosecution has not established an unbroken chain of custody for the impugned mortar samples from collection to laboratory analysis. In absence of documentary continuity regarding sealing, transit, custody transfer, and laboratory receipt, the forensic opinion cannot be treated as a reliable incriminating foundation at the stage of charge.

### Para 2 - representative sampling
The record indicates collection in adverse rainy conditions and by non-systematic method. Such collection does not satisfy representative sampling logic for cementitious material and materially increases the risk of contamination and distortion, thereby weakening the probative value of test outcomes.

### Para 3 - fair procedure
Collection and testing were not shown to have been conducted with fair participation opportunity to the accused contractor side. This procedural deficit prejudices defence rights and warrants strict judicial scrutiny of subsequent laboratory conclusions.

### Para 4 - expert evidence caution
Expert opinion under Section 45 is only as credible as the integrity of source material. Where sampling and custody foundations are disputed or undocumented, the opinion must be evaluated as unsafe for criminal liability inference.

### Para 5 - causation burden
In a criminal negligence prosecution, the State must prove proximate causation beyond suspicion. A disputed forensic report, without procedural integrity and without exclusion of alternate causes, does not satisfy that threshold.
