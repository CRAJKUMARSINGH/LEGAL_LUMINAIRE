# CASE_03 — Discharge Application — NI Act §138
## Metropolitan Magistrate Court, Delhi

**FIR/Complaint:** CC No. 1892/2023, MM Court Saket, Delhi
**Accused:** Sunil Agarwal, Director, M/s Sunrise Traders Pvt. Ltd.
**Charges:** NI Act §138 — dishonour of cheque Rs. 15 lakhs
**Court:** MM Court, Saket, Delhi

## Facts
Cheque No. 004521 dated 10-01-2023 for Rs. 15,00,000 drawn on HDFC Bank.
Returned with memo "Funds Insufficient" on 15-01-2023.
Complainant: M/s Apex Suppliers Pvt. Ltd.
Accused claims: cheque was given as security, not for discharge of debt.
No legally enforceable debt exists — transaction was a loan with disputed terms.
Statutory notice sent 25-01-2023. Reply sent 05-02-2023 disputing liability.

## Defence Strategy
1. Cheque given as security — not for discharge of legally enforceable debt
2. No legally enforceable debt — disputed loan terms
3. Notice reply sent within time — dispute raised
4. Presumption u/s 139 rebutted by evidence of security nature
5. Rangappa v. Sri Mohan (2010) 11 SCC 441 — presumption rebuttable
6. Krishna Janardhan Bhat v. Dattatraya (2008) 4 SCC 54 — security cheque

## Expected Output
- Discharge application u/s 245 CrPC / §250 BNSS
- Grounds: security cheque, no legally enforceable debt, presumption rebutted
- Precedents: Rangappa (2010), Krishna Janardhan Bhat (2008)
- Cross-reference matrix
- Pre-filing checklist
