# CASE_05 — Bail Application — Cyber Fraud
## Sessions Court, Mumbai

**FIR:** 1247/2024 dated 20-06-2024, PS Cyber Crime, Mumbai
**Accused:** Priya Nair, Software Engineer, Mumbai
**Charges:** IT Act §66C (identity theft), §66D (cheating by impersonation) + IPC 420, 468
**Court:** Sessions Court, Mumbai

## Facts
Accused allegedly created fake social media profiles of a businessman.
Used profiles to solicit Rs. 45 lakhs from victims promising investment returns.
Digital forensics: IP address traced to accused's home WiFi.
Accused claims: WiFi was open/shared — IP address not conclusive.
No direct financial transaction from accused's bank account.
No physical evidence linking accused to the scheme.

## Defence Strategy
1. IP address not conclusive — open WiFi, multiple users
2. No financial trail — no transaction from accused's accounts
3. Digital forensics report — chain of custody of digital evidence not established
4. Circumstantial evidence only — no direct proof
5. Shreya Singhal v. Union of India (2015) 5 SCC 1 — digital evidence standards
6. Anvar P.V. v. P.K. Basheer (2014) 10 SCC 473 — §65B certificate mandatory

## Expected Output
- Bail application u/s 439 CrPC
- Grounds: IP not conclusive, no financial trail, digital evidence CoC failure
- Precedents: Anvar P.V. (2014), Shreya Singhal (2015)
- Verification report
- Pre-filing checklist
