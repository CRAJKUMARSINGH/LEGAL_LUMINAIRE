## RAJASTHAN HIGH COURT (RHC) CITATIONS

### 1. RAJASTHAN HIGH COURT - 2008 JAIPUR SERIAL BLASTS CASE (2023)

**Citation**: Rajasthan High Court, March 29, 2023  
**Judges**: Division Bench - Justices Pankaj Bhandari and Sameer Jain  
**Source**: [The Hindu Report](https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/other-states/rajasthan-hc-acquits-all-accused-in-deadly-2008-jaipur-blasts/article66676139.ece)

**Case Facts**:
- Serial bomb blasts in Jaipur (May 13, 2008) killed 71 people, injured 200
- Trial court awarded death sentence to 4 accused in December 2019
- High Court acquitted all accused on appeal

**Key Holdings for Our Defence**:

> **"Fabricated Evidence"**: "Some of the evidence produced against the accused also appeared to be fabricated"

> **"Failed to Prove Evidence"**: "The Anti-Terrorism Squad (ATS) of the Rajasthan police had failed to prove any aspect of the evidence and there was no basis to establish its theory of conspiracy"

> **"Conviction Cannot Rest on Suspicion"**: "Though it was cognizant of the heinousness of the crime, a conviction could not be recorded in the absence of any serious investigation and proof as per law. The more heinous the crime, the stricter should be the scrutiny"

> **"Inadmissible Statements"**: "Since the statements made by the accused to the police officers were inadmissible as evidence, they could not form the basis for their conviction"

**Critical Findings**:
- **Insufficient Evidence**: "There was no evidence to prove the allegation of travel of the accused between Delhi and Jaipur and their purchase of the bicycles"
- **Unreliable Identification**: "The identification of the accused by key witnesses was not reliable"
- **Material Witnesses Not Examined**: "Several material witnesses were not examined"
- **Inadmissible Electronic Evidence**: "The e-mail allegedly sent taking responsibility for the blasts was inadmissible in law"

**Direct Application to Stadium Case**:
- Fabricated or unreliable forensic evidence
- Failure to prove evidence according to law
- Conviction cannot rest on suspicion alone
- Stricter scrutiny required for serious cases

## OTHER HIGH COURT CITATIONS - BUILDING COLLAPSE CASES

### 4. GUJARAT EARTHQUAKE BUILDING COLLAPSE - AHMEDABAD COURT (2026)

**Citation**: Chief Judicial Magistrate, Ahmedabad Rural Court, J.A. Baxi  
**Case**: Builder Rashmin Patel and Contractor Bhupendra Patel  
**Source**: [Times of India Report](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/ahmedabad/2001-gujarat-earthquake-court-acquits-builder-contractor-in-building-collapse-case/articleshow/128172502.cms)

**Case Facts**:
- Building collapse during 2001 Gujarat earthquake
- 2 people killed in Sarvadarshan Apartments collapse
- Builder and contractor charged under IPC 304, 418, 420

**Critical Forensic Evidence Failure**:
> **"No Forensic Analysis Record"**: "Although samples were collected from the debris, there was no record that a forensic analysis was done to establish that the construction material was substandard"

> **"No Evidence of Poor Quality"**: "The court did not find evidence of charges that the construction was of poor quality"

> **"No Record of Insufficient Materials"**: "There is no record showing that Bhupendra, the builder, used fewer or insufficient materials than necessary in the construction"

**Acquittal Reasoning**:
- Samples collected but no forensic analysis conducted
- No record of substandard materials
- No evidence of construction defects
- Natural disaster (earthquake) as primary cause

**Direct Application to Stadium Case**:
- Sample collection without proper analysis is insufficient
- Burden on prosecution to establish material defects through forensic evidence
- Lack of forensic analysis records leads to acquittal

### 5. DELHI COURT - CEILING COLLAPSE CASE (2026)

**Citation**: JMFC Pankaj Rai, Karkardooma Court, Delhi, February 20, 2026  
**Case**: Shiv Dutt (Building Owner) - 27-year-old case  
**Source**: [Indian Express Report](https://indianexpress.com/article/legal-news/delhi-court-acquits-building-owner-1999-ceiling-collapse-case-10568220/)

**Case Facts**:
- Ceiling collapse during construction (September 1999)
- 1 labourer killed, 7 injured
- Building owner charged with negligence

**Key Holdings for Our Defence**:
> **"Owner Not Liable for Contractor's Acts"**: "Property owner cannot be held liable for the 'rash and negligent act' of an independent contractor hired to execute the work"

> **"Direct Nexus Required"**: "There must be proof that the rash or negligent act of the accused was the proximate cause of death. There must be a direct nexus between the death of the person and the rash and negligent act of the accused"

> **"No Supervision Evidence"**: "None of the PWs (witnesses) have deposed in their evidence that they ever witnessed the accused Shiv Dutt at the site and that construction was going on under his supervision and control"

**Application**: Establishes principle that contractors cannot be held liable without direct evidence of their negligent acts and supervision.

## FORENSIC EVIDENCE FAILURE PATTERNS

### Common Reasons for Acquittals in Building Cases:

1. **No Forensic Analysis Conducted**
   - Gujarat case: Samples collected but no analysis done
   - Stadium case parallel: Improper sampling negates analysis

2. **Fabricated or Unreliable Evidence**
   - Rajasthan HC: "Evidence appeared to be fabricated"
   - Stadium case parallel: Rain-contaminated samples unreliable

3. **Failure to Establish Chain of Evidence**
   - Multiple cases show evidence chain failures
   - Stadium case parallel: No chain of custody documentation

4. **Insufficient Evidence Standards**
   - Courts require "strong and cogent evidence"
   - Stadium case parallel: Contaminated samples insufficient

5. **No Direct Nexus Established**
   - Delhi case: No direct connection between accused and negligence
   - Stadium case parallel: No proper evidence linking contractor to defects

**Application**: Stadium collapse being serious matter requires strictest evidence standards.

**Application**: Improper sampling in stadium case negates forensic analysis value.

**Application**: Stadium case lacks any chain of custody documentation.

**Consistent Principle**: Prosecution must establish guilt beyond reasonable doubt with reliable evidence

**Application**: Contaminated samples cannot meet this burden.
