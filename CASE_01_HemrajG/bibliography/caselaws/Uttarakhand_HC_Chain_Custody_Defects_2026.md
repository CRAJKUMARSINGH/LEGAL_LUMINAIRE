# UTTARAKHAND HIGH COURT - CHAIN OF CUSTODY DEFECTS CASE (2026)
## Rape Conviction Cannot Rest On Suspicion; Chain Of Custody Defects Undermine Forensic Evidence

**Date**: March 28, 2026  
**Court**: Uttarakhand High Court  
**Source**: [LiveLaw Report](https://www.livelaw.in/high-court/uttarakhand-high-court/uttarakhand-high-court-rape-conviction-chain-of-custody-forensic-evidence-528106)

## KEY HOLDING - CRITICAL FOR OUR DEFENCE

> **"A conviction must rest on legally proved evidence and not suspicion, however strong, and that in the absence of a duly established chain of custody, forensic evidence loses its evidentiary value and cannot be treated as conclusive."**

## CASE SUMMARY

**Facts**: 
- Rape conviction case
- Forensic evidence challenged on chain of custody grounds  
- Prosecution failed to establish proper evidence handling procedures

**Legal Issue**: Whether forensic evidence without proper chain of custody can sustain criminal conviction

**Holding**: Court set aside convictions of appellants-accused due to chain of custody defects in forensic evidence

## DIRECT APPLICATION TO STADIUM COLLAPSE CASE

### Legal Principle Established:
1. **Evidence Standard**: Conviction must rest on "legally proved evidence" not suspicion
2. **Chain of Custody Mandatory**: Absence of duly established chain of custody renders forensic evidence inadmissible
3. **Evidentiary Value Lost**: Forensic evidence without proper chain of custody "loses its evidentiary value"
4. **Not Conclusive**: Such evidence "cannot be treated as conclusive"

### Application to Mortar Sampling:
1. **No Chain of Custody**: Stadium case lacks any documentation of sample handling from collection to testing
2. **Suspicion vs Proof**: Prosecution relying on suspicion rather than legally proved evidence
3. **Forensic Report Challenge**: Mortar testing report should lose evidentiary value due to chain of custody defects
4. **Burden on Prosecution**: Must establish proper evidence handling procedures

## LEGAL ARGUMENT FRAMEWORK

**Primary Argument**: Following Uttarakhand High Court precedent, the forensic mortar testing report in the stadium collapse case should be held inadmissible due to complete absence of chain of custody documentation.

**Supporting Points**:
1. No record of who collected samples, when, or under what conditions
2. No documentation of storage, sealing, or transport procedures  
3. No evidence of proper handling during laboratory testing
4. Contractor denied right to have representative present during critical evidence collection

**Legal Standard**: Court must reject forensic evidence that fails to meet chain of custody requirements, as such evidence "loses its evidentiary value and cannot be treated as conclusive"
